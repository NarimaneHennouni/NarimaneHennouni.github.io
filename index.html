ARDD

Automated Road Defects Detection, known as ARDD is a device that can be laced in any car's dashboard to detect defects along the road. It uses a computer vision model trained on Algerian data, collected by us, and deployed on a Jetson Nano by NVIDIA. It automatically detects defects along the road and displays the results and statistics on the associated mobile app.
This was our participation in the first version of Algeria AI Challenge in which we won the 2nd place and also won the 1st place in Algeria Disrupt, a national event for startups.
There is an article about our project and our participation in the event written by U.S.A. Herald you can find it in this link https://usaherald.com/how-artificial-intelligence-is-changing-algeria-and-africa/

Salamat

Part of Summer Break Challenge organized by Djezzy and Africa By IncubMe, we participated in this competition representing WomenTech Makers Algiers. Our idea was to provide an AI based solution to help people better respond during natural disasters. Especially that last summer Algeria went through a series of fire and many people were affected.Our main goal is to connect those in need with those willing to help through a social media like platform dedicated only for this, and allows to send SOS signals even offline using BLE technology. The app is also powered by a chatbot to better assist people in their request for help

Sirius GPS

Sirius GPS was my internship project which I worked for 6 weeks last year in a company called Sirius NET.
The project consists of developing a mobile application using Flutter ( a technology that is so exciting to learn and work with), in order to allow the supervision of personal and business vehicules (fleet management), the application is connected to a web server for information retrieval. The main function of Sirius GPS is that it allows you to track all your registered vehicles in real time, check their position, speed, battery level as well as if they exceeded the speed limit or not.
